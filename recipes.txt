Classic, oven baked version of your favorite middle eastern chick pea ball. With a large food processor, this is simplicity to make!

I am one of the people to whom cilantro tastes like soap, so I never cook with it.

Adapted from the fantastic toriavey recipe.

Ingredients

2 cans chickpeas (approx 15 oz each)
1 small onion, minced
1 Tbs dried parsley
3 cloves garlic 
1 ½ tbsp flour
1 ¾ tsp salt
2 tsp cumin
1 tsp ground coriander
¼ tsp black pepper
¼ tsp cayenne pepper

Directions

Preheat oven to 400F

Just in time for (Canadian) Thanksgiving, this apple pie is very straightforward to make with the only time consuming step being peeling and coring apples. If you have a hand operated apple peeler/corer it will save you tons of time. 

Shout-out to my Little Sister, who helped prepare the apples used in this pie. Big Brothers Big Sisters is an amazing organization!

20 minutes preparation, 30 minutes cooking time

Ingredients

Pie

1 package cream cheese, softened
1 cup butter, softened
3 ¼ cups flour, divided (3 cups, ¼ cup)
10 Granny Smith apples, peeled cored and sliced into small sections
1 ¼ cups sugar
1 tsp. ground cinnamon
½ tsp nutmeg

Glaze

¾ tsp powdered sugar
1 Tbs. water

Directions

Preheat oven to 400 F
Beat cream cheese and butter in large bowl until well blended
Gradually add 3 cups flour, mixing on low after each addition until just blended
Shape dough into 2 balls, place one on large lightly floured sheet of waxed paper
Cover with second floured sheet of wax paper and roll out to 15x10" rectangle. Discard second sheet of wax paper. 
Gently press onto bottom and sides of a rectangular pan
Toss apples with remaining flour, sugar, cinnamon and nutmeg, spread onto crust
Repeat rolling out of remaining dough ball to make second rectangle, or any other shapes you wish (lattice, hearts, etc).
Carefully place dough on top of apples, tucking edges together. Make several  steam vent slits in top crust
Bake in middle rack of oven for 30 minutes or until crust is golden brown
Meanwhile, mix powered sugar and water to form glaze.
Cool dessert 15 minutes before drizzling glaze over top. Cool 30 minutes before cutting into bars.

Notes:

PRO TIP: For that lovely golden colored crust, brush with egg white prior to baking
PRO TIP: The choice of apples here is very important. I firmly believe you can make pie with any type of apple, but if you try any SWEET variety with this recipe it will likely be too sweet
PRO TIP: Forgot to soften your butter? Hit it in the microwave for 30 seconds or until you see a SMALL amount of liquid butter at the bottom. While not ideal for some recipes, it will work for this one :)

Yesterday while browsing my recipes and deciding what to make, I remembered one thing I’d been trying to do is make a thicker chicken noodle soup. Inspiration struck - I could use the roux from the ham soup! That is how this was born - another original recipe! (Though, to be honest, it is Frankenstein’d from other recipes)

20 minutes preparation, 20 minutes cooking time

Ingredients

3 cups potatoes, chopped
4 carrots, chopped into quarter rounds
(Optional) 6 asparagus spears, chopped
2 cups uncooked chicken breast meat, cut into small pieces
6 cups water
2 Tbs chicken base
1 tsp salt
1 tsp black pepper
2 Tbs Chicken Noodle Soup Seasoning
10 Tbs butter (approx. ½ cup)
10 Tbs flour (approx. 1/3 cup)

Directions

This freezes exceptionally well, so I usually make a huge pot of it. Lots of variation is possible, but I recommend not reducing the cooking time - 2 hours leaves the beef melt-in-your-mouth tender. Tons of notes at the bottom!

Time

15 minutes preparation, 2 hours 10 minutes cooking time

Ingredients

2 lbs stewing beef cut into 1" chunks
1 tsp garlic salt
½ tsp black pepper
3 tbs vegetable oil
3 cups water
4 tsp beef base (or beef bouillon - I use Costco beef base)
1 cup red wine (Merlot is great)
1 tsp dried parsley
3 red potatoes cut into 1" chunks
3 carrots cut into 1" chunks
3 tbs corn starch
3 tbs water

Directions

Season beef with garlic salt and pepper, then brown quickly on high heat with oil in large dutch oven or pot (The beef will stick to the bottom and make a mess - this is expected!)
Add beef base, water, wine and parsley to pot and stir
Bring to boil then cover and simmer on low heat for 1 hour
Add potatoes and carrots to pot, stir
Combine corn starch and water and add to pot, stir
Simmer 1 hour, serve

Notes:

Onion-friendly: add 1 cup chopped onions in step 4 with carrots and potatoes
The choice of potatoes is important - russet potatoes will turn to mush, but red potatoes will keep their shape 
I tend to leave this dish unsalted as it is VERY easy to over-salt, especially if your beef base is high in sodium
If you like a thicker stew, add ¼ cup flour to beef when browning

Description

I think this is my best recipe - the one I experiment the most with. I call it a "stew" because it ends up having very little liquid, and that is how we like it!

Time

20 minutes preparation time, 40 minutes cooking time

Ingredients

1 store-bought roast chicken
3 L chicken broth
5 large carrots cut into rounds or half rounds
(Optional) 2 large stalks celery, sliced
1 tbs Chicken Noodle Soup Seasoning
1 package egg noodles

Directions

Separate chicken meat from bones, reserve meat
Bring to a boil chicken broth with chicken bones, simmer 30 minutes or longer (longer means more flavor)
Using a slotted spoon and a scoop with fine holes, remove and discard chicken from broth
Bring broth to boil and add remaining ingredients
After 10 minutes, enjoy!

Notes:

For more of a "soup", use ½ package egg noodles
For a "thicker" soup, chop all bones into small pieces to release marrow, a thickening agent
Like onions? Add ½ cup chopped onions in step 4

One of my husband’s favorite recipes, the breadcrumbs give it a fantastic crunch. This is a great recipe for experimentation - it is easy to predict how the final result will taste. Throw all your favorite ingredients in!

Time

10 minutes preparation time, 20 minutes cooking time

Ingredients

1 large head cauliflower, broken into small florets
1 large head broccoli, broken into small florets
½ cup butter
¼ cup Parmesan cheese
2/3 cup Melissa’s Crumb mix (or 2/3 cup seasoned bread crumbs)
1 tsp salt
1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Directions

Preheat oven to 350 F and grease a 9x11" pan
Place broccoli and cauliflower In microwave safe bowl, microwave on High for 3 minutes, place in pan
Melt butter in microwave
Add breadcrumbs, Parmesan and salt to butter and pour over broccoli and cauliflower, top with Cheddar cheese
Bake in the preheated oven for 20 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly

Notes:

Alternatively; steam broccoli and cauliflower 

Melissa’s Crumb Mix: Combine equal parts seasoned dry bread crumbs, puffed rice cereal and crushed buttery crackers (Ritz). This mixture works great for any breaded meat recipe. For taste, add finely shredded cheddar and Parmesan cheese.

Time

30 minutes preparation time, 30 minutes cooking time

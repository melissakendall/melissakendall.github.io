45 minutes preparation, 20 minutes cooking time

**Description**

Amazing potstickers.

Be warned, this recipe is simple but messy and time consuming.

Adapted from the fabulous Alton Brown’s Perfect Potstickers

**Ingredients**

* 1 lb ground pork
* 2 eggs
* 1 cup finely chopped cabbage
* 1 cup finely chopped mushrooms (variety, or shitake) 
* 1 clove garlic, minced
* 1 Tbs ketchup
* 1 Tbs hoisin sauce
* 1 Tbs Worcestershire sauce
* 1 tsp brown sugar
* ½ tsp black pepper
* ½ tsp salt
* ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
* 60 wonton wrappers
* 3-4 Tbs vegetable oil
* 2 cups chicken stock

**Directions**

1. Combine first 12 Ingredients (pork to cayenne pepper) in a large mixing bowl. Set aside.
2. Form dumplings: Place 1 tsp pork mixture in wonton wrapper, brush edges with water and fold, sealing edges and shaping as desired 
3. Set on a sheet pan and cover with damp cloth. Ensure wonton wrappers are also placed under damp cloth so they do not harden
4. Heat a pan over medium heat. Brush with vegetable oil when hot.
5. In batches of 8-10, place pot stickers in pan for 2 minutes (don’t touch!) then carefully add 1/3 cup chicken stock, cover and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
6. When batches are done, place in heat-safe pan and cover with tin foil
7. Between batches, clean pan by de-glazing; add water and allow to boil

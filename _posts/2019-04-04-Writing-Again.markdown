Camp NaNoWriMo has begun, as of April 1st!

I am officially 5000 words into my second novel. This one is suspense/thriller romance with a focus on technology. 

**Preview** (An early draft, scene probably won't make final draft)

"What are you doing here, anyway? It's late." Peter said.

Sandra pulled a piece of black plastic from her pocket, rectangular with a metal mouth. A USB drive.

"I found this."

"What is it?"

She turned the drive over to expose a switch on the side. "It's a penetration tool."

"A what?"

"A tool used for testing the security of a network. Basically."

She swirled the drive in her fingers, around and around it went.

"Our security?" That was a concern, if someone was trying to hack them. Waterpoint had no sensitive client information stored in their records, just architectural diagrams. Blueprints. Useful to criminals in very select situations, maybe, but to the public?

"Ours." Sandra agreed, flicking the switch up and down. "Armed. Not armed. Armed. That's how I found it, on the armed setting. A neat little tool, if a bit lazy. I prefer old school methods."

"What are you going to do?"

A smile spread across her face. "Set a trap. Catch our little pen tester in the act. Shouldn't be too hard."

Peter struggled to understand her words with half a bottle of bourbon clouding his thoughts.

"Wouldn't you... tell someone? It's a crime, isn't it?"

She laughed, and the sound was unexpectedly delicate and sweet.

"For this? No, this is the work of an amateur. He left it in my station - and not even in one of the back ports where I might overlook it - right in the front, jutting out like a broken tooth. Frankly, I'm insulted."
